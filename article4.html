Payment Guidelines

Follow these safe payment practices to protect yourself:

  1. Deposit After Confirmation: It's acceptable to provide a small deposit (10-20% maximum) after you've verified the welder's identity and have seen some of their previous work. Never pay more than 20% upfront.
  2. Written Agreement: Before starting the job, always get a simple written agreement detailing the scope of work and costs involved. This will protect both parties and clarify expectations. Include payment milestones tied to work completion.
  3. Milestone Payments: Structure payments based on project milestones (e.g., 20% to start, 30% at midpoint, 50% upon completion and inspection).
  4. Final Payment After Inspection: Never release the final payment until you've thoroughly inspected the work and confirmed it meets agreed-upon standards. Use the AI Defector tool to verify quality.

Spotting Fake Payment Requests

It's crucial to remain vigilant while hiring welders. Here are some tips for identifying suspicious payment requests:

üö´ Common Scam Tactics

  • Fake Representation: Be wary of welders who claim they represent Welder's Kit and request payments via links or request your complete upfront payment. Welder's Kit NEVER collects payments.
  • Urgent Payment Demands: Scammers create false urgency, claiming you must pay immediately to secure services or materials.
  • Payment Links: Never click on payment links sent via WhatsApp, email, or SMS claiming to be from Welder's Kit or any welder.
  • "Processing Fees": If someone asks for "processing fees," "registration fees," or "verification fees" to hire a welder, it's a scam.
  • Unofficial Payment Methods: Be cautious of requests for payment via gift cards, cryptocurrency, or wire transfers to unknown accounts.
